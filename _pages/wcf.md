## CHAPTER 1: Of the Holy Scripture
<p><span id="wcf-1-1"></span><a href="#wcf-1-1-proofs" class="section-num">1.</a> Although the light of nature, and the works of creation and providence do so far manifest the goodness, wisdom, and power of God, as to leave men unexcusable;<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup> yet are they not sufficient to give that knowledge of God, and of his will, which is necessary unto salvation.<sup class="proof-marker">b</sup> Therefore it pleased the Lord, at sundry times, and in divers manners, to reveal himself, and to declare that his will unto his church;<sup class="proof-marker">c</sup> and afterwards, for the better preserving and propagating of the truth, and for the more sure establishment and comfort of the church against the corruption of the flesh, and the malice of Satan and of the world, to commit the same wholly unto writing:<sup class="proof-marker">d</sup> which maketh the Holy Scripture to be most necessary;<sup class="proof-marker">e</sup> those former ways of God's revealing his will unto his people being now ceased.<sup class="proof-marker">f</sup></p>

## CHAPTER 2 Of God, and of the Holy Trinity
<p><span id="wcf-2-1"></span><a href="#wcf-2-1-proofs" class="section-num">1.</a> There is but one only,<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup> living, and true God,<sup class="proof-marker">b</sup> who is infinite in being and perfection,<sup class="proof-marker">c</sup> a most pure spirit,<sup class="proof-marker">d</sup> invisible,<sup class="proof-marker">e</sup> without body, parts,<sup class="proof-marker">f</sup> or passions;<sup class="proof-marker">g</sup> immutable,<sup class="proof-marker">h</sup> immense,<sup class="proof-marker">i</sup> eternal, incomprehensible,<sup class="proof-marker">l</sup> almighty,<sup class="proof-marker">m</sup> most wise,<sup class="proof-marker">n</sup> most holy,<sup class="proof-marker">o</sup> most free,<sup class="proof-marker">p</sup> most absolute;<sup class="proof-marker">q</sup> working all things according to the counsel of his own immutable and most righteous will,<sup class="proof-marker">r</sup> for his own glory;<sup class="proof-marker">s</sup> most loving,<sup class="proof-marker">t</sup> gracious, merciful, long-suffering, abundant in goodness and truth, forgiving iniquity, transgression, and sin;<sup class="proof-marker">u</sup> the rewarder of them that diligently seek him;<sup class="proof-marker">w</sup> and withal, most just, and terrible in his judgments,<sup class="proof-marker">x</sup> hating all sin,<sup class="proof-marker">y</sup> and who will by no means clear the guilty.<sup class="proof-marker">z</sup></p>

<p><span id="wcf-2-2"></span><a href="#wcf-2-2-proofs" class="section-num">2.</a> God hath all life,<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup> glory,<sup class="proof-marker">b</sup> goodness,<sup class="proof-marker">c</sup> blessedness,<sup class="proof-marker">d</sup> in and of himself; and is alone in and unto himself all-sufficient, not standing in need of any creatures which he hath made,<sup class="proof-marker">e</sup> nor deriving any glory from them,<sup class="proof-marker">f</sup> but only manifesting his own glory in, by, unto, and upon them. ## CHAPTER 3: Of God's Eternal Decree
<p><span id="wcf-3-1"></span><a href="#wcf-3-1-proofs" class="section-num">1.</a> God, from all eternity, did, by the most wise and holy counsel of his own will, freely, and unchangeably ordain whatsoever comes to pass:<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup> yet so, as thereby neither is God the author of sin,<sup class="proof-marker">b</sup> nor is violence offered to the will of the creatures; nor is the liberty or contingency of second causes taken away, but rather established.<sup class="proof-marker">c</sup></p>

## CHAPTER 4: Of Creation
<p><span id="wcf-4-1"></span><a href="#wcf-4-1-proofs" class="section-num">1.</a> It pleased God the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost,<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup> for the manifestation of the glory of his eternal power, wisdom, and goodness,<sup class="proof-marker">b</sup> in the beginning, to create, or make of nothing, the world, and all things therein whether visible or invisible, in the space of six days; and all very good.<sup class="proof-marker">c</sup></p>

## CHAPTER 18: Of the Assurance of Grace and Salvation
<p><span id="wcf-18-1"></span><a href="#wcf-18-1-proofs" class="section-num">1.</a> Although hypocrites and other unregenerate men may vainly deceive themselves with false hopes and carnal presumptions of being in the favor of God, and estate of salvation (which hope of theirs shall perish): yet such as truly believe in the Lord Jesus, and love him in sincerity, endeavoring to walk in all good conscience before him, may, in this life, be certainly assured that they are in the state of grace,<sup class="proof-marker">c</sup> and may rejoice in the hope of the glory of God, which hope shall never make them ashamed.<sup class="proof-marker">d</sup></p>

## CHAPTER 20: Of Christian Liberty, and Liberty of Conscience
<p><span id="wcf-20-1"></span><a href="#wcf-20-1-proofs" class="section-num">1.</a> The liberty which Christ hath purchased for believers under the gospel consists in their freedom from the guilt of sin, the condemning wrath of God, the curse of the moral law;<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup> and, in their being delivered from this present evil world, bondage to Satan, and dominion of sin;<sup class="proof-marker">b</sup> from the evil of afflictions, the sting of death, the victory of the grave, and everlasting damnation;<sup class="proof-marker">c</sup> as also, in their free access to God,<sup class="proof-marker">d</sup> and their yielding obedience unto him, not out of slavish fear, but a childlike love and willing mind.<sup class="proof-marker">e</sup> All which were common also to believers under the law.<sup class="proof-marker">f</sup> But, under the new testament, the liberty of Christians is further enlarged, in their freedom from the yoke of the ceremonial law, to which the Jewish church was subjected;<sup class="proof-marker">g</sup> and in greater boldness of access to the throne of grace,<sup class="proof-marker">h</sup> and in fuller communications of the free Spirit of God, than believers under the law did ordinarily partake of.<sup class="proof-marker">i</sup></p>

## CHAPTER 28: Of Baptism
<p><span id="wcf-28-1"></span><a href="#wcf-28-1-proofs" class="section-num">1.</a> Baptism is a sacrament of the new testament, ordained by Jesus Christ,<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup> not only for the solemn admission of the party baptized into the visible church;<sup class="proof-marker">b</sup> but also, to be unto him a sign and seal of the covenant of grace,<sup class="proof-marker">c</sup> of his ingrafting into Christ,<sup class="proof-marker">d</sup> of regeneration,<sup class="proof-marker">e</sup> of remission of sins,<sup class="proof-marker">f</sup> and of his giving up unto God, through Jesus Christ, to walk in newness of life.<sup class="proof-marker">g</sup> Which sacrament is, by Christ's own appointment, to be continued in his church until the end of the world.<sup class="proof-marker">h</sup></p>

## CHAPTER 29: Of the Lord's Supper
<p><span id="wcf-29-1"></span><a href="#wcf-29-1-proofs" class="section-num">1.</a> Our Lord Jesus, in the night wherein he was betrayed, instituted the sacrament of his body and blood, called the Lord's Supper, to be observed in his church, unto the end of the world, for the perpetual remembrance of the sacrifice of himself in his death; the sealing all benefits thereof unto true believers, their spiritual nourishment and growth in him, their further engagement in and to all duties which they owe unto him; and, to be a bond and pledge of their communion with him, and with each other, as members of his mystical body.<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup></p>

## CHAPTER 32: Of the State of Men after Death, and of the Resurrection of the Dead
<p><span id="wcf-32-1"></span><a href="#wcf-32-1-proofs" class="section-num">1.</a> The bodies of men, after death, return to dust, and see corruption:<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup> but their souls, which neither die nor sleep, having an immortal subsistence, immediately return to God who gave them:<sup class="proof-marker">b</sup> the souls of the righteous, being then made perfect in holiness, are received into the highest heavens, where they behold the face of God, in light and glory, waiting for the full redemption of their bodies.<sup class="proof-marker">c</sup> And the souls of the wicked are cast into hell, where they remain in torments and utter darkness, reserved to the judgment of the great day.<sup class="proof-marker">d</sup> Besides these two places, for souls separated from their bodies, the Scripture acknowledgeth none.</p>

## CHAPTER 33: Of the Last Judgment
<p><span id="wcf-33-1"></span><a href="#wcf-33-1-proofs" class="section-num">1.</a> God hath appointed a day, wherein he will judge the world, in righteousness, by Jesus Christ,<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup> to whom all power and judgment is given of the Father.<sup class="proof-marker">b</sup> In which day, not only the apostate angels shall be judged,<sup class="proof-marker">c</sup> but likewise all persons that have lived upon earth shall appear before the tribunal of Christ, to give an account of their thoughts, words, and deeds; and to receive according to what they have done in the body, whether good or evil.<sup class="proof-marker">d</sup></p>

</details>

<p><span id="wcf-33-2"></span><a href="#wcf-33-2-proofs" class="section-num">2.</a> The end of God's appointing this day is for the manifestation of the glory of his mercy, in the eternal salvation of the elect; and of his justice, in the damnation of the reprobate, who are wicked and disobedient. For then shall the righteous go into everlasting life, and receive that fullness of joy and refreshing, which shall come from the presence of the Lord; but the wicked who know not God, and obey not the gospel of Jesus Christ, shall be cast into eternal torments, and be punished with everlasting destruction from the presence of the Lord, and from the glory of his power.<sup class="proof-marker">e</sup></p>
