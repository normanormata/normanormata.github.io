Answer: Man's chief and highest end is to glorify God,<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup> and fully to enjoy him forever.<sup class="proof-marker">b</sup>

### Question 2: How doth it appear that there is a God?
Answer: The very light of nature in man, and the works of God, declare plainly that there is a God;<sup class="proof-marker">c</sup> but his word and Spirit only do sufficiently and effectually reveal him unto men for their salvation.<sup class="proof-marker">d</sup>

Answer: The Holy Scriptures of the Old and New Testament are the Word of God,<sup class="proof-marker">e</sup> the only rule of faith and obedience.<sup class="proof-marker">f</sup>

### Question 4: How doth it appear that the Scriptures are the Word of God?
Answer: The Scriptures manifest themselves to be the Word of God, by their majesty and purity;<sup class="proof-marker">h</sup> by the consent of all the parts,<sup class="proof-marker">i</sup> and the scope of the whole, which is to give all glory to God; by their light and power to convince and convert sinners, to comfort and build up believers unto salvation:<sup class="proof-marker">l</sup> but the Spirit of God bearing witness by and with the Scriptures in the heart of man, is alone able fully to persuade it that they are the very Word of God.<sup class="proof-marker">m</sup>

Answer: There be three persons in the Godhead, the Father, the Son, and the Holy Ghost;<sup class="proof-marker">n</sup> and these three are one true, eternal God, the same in substance, equal in power and glory; although distinguished by their personal properties.<sup class="proof-marker">o</sup>

Answer: God's decrees are the wise, free, and holy acts of the counsel of his will,<sup class="proof-marker">x</sup> whereby, from all eternity, he hath, for his own glory, unchangeably foreordained whatsoever comes to pass in time,<sup class="proof-marker">y</sup> especially concerning angels and men.

### Question 13: What hath God especially decreed concerning angels and men?
Answer: God, by an eternal and immutable decree, out of his mere love, for the praise of his glorious grace, to be manifested in due time, hath elected some angels to glory;<sup class="proof-marker">z</sup> and in Christ hath chosen some men to eternal life, and the means thereof:<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup> and also, according to his sovereign power, and the unsearchable counsel of his own will (whereby he extendeth or withholdeth favor as he pleaseth), hath passed by and foreordained the rest to dishonor and wrath, to be for their sin inflicted, to the praise of the glory of his justice.<sup class="proof-marker">b</sup>

### Question 99: What rules are to be observed for the right understanding of the Ten Commandments?
Answer: For the right understanding of the Ten Commandments, these rules are to be observed:
1. That the law is perfect, and bindeth every one to full conformity in the whole man unto the righteousness thereof, and unto entire obedience forever; so as to require the utmost perfection of every duty, and to forbid the least degree of every sin.<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup>
2. That it is spiritual, and so reacheth the understanding, will, affections, and all other powers of the soul; as well as words, works, and gestures.<sup class="proof-marker">b</sup>
3. That one and the same thing, in divers respects, is required or forbidden in several commandments.
4. That as, where a duty is commanded, the contrary sin is forbidden; and, where a sin is forbidden, the contrary duty is commanded: so, where a promise is annexed, the contrary threatening is included; and, where a threatening is annexed, the contrary promise is included.
5. That what God forbids, is at no time to be done;<sup class="proof-marker">h</sup> what he commands, is always our duty;<sup class="proof-marker">i</sup> and yet every particular duty is not to be done at all times.
6. That under one sin or duty, all of the same kind are forbidden or commanded; together with all the causes, means, occasions, and appearances thereof, and provocations thereunto.<sup class="proof-marker">l</sup>
7. That what is forbidden or commanded to ourselves, we are bound, according to our places, to endeavor that it may be avoided or performed by others, according to the duty of their places.<sup class="proof-marker">m</sup>
8. That in what is commanded to others, we are bound, according to our places and callings, to be helpful to them;<sup class="proof-marker">n</sup> and to take heed of partaking with others in what is forbidden them.<sup class="proof-marker">o</sup>

Answer: Sins receive their aggravations,
1. From the persons offending; if they be of riper age,<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup> greater experience or grace, eminent for profession, gifts, place, office, guides to others, and whose example is likely to be followed by others.

From circumstances of time, and place:<sup class="proof-marker">e</sup> if on the Lord's day, or other times of divine worship;<sup class="proof-marker">g</sup> or immediately before or after these,<sup class="proof-marker">i</sup> or other helps to prevent or remedy such miscarriages: if in public, or in the presence of others, who are thereby likely to be provoked or defiled.<sup class="proof-marker">l</sup>

Answer: In the third petition (which is, Thy will be done in earth, as it is in heaven), acknowledging that by nature we and all men are not only utterly unable and unwilling to know and to do the will of God,<sup class="proof-marker">b</sup> but prone to rebel against his word,<sup class="proof-marker">c</sup> to repine and murmur against his providence,<sup class="proof-marker">d</sup> and wholly inclined to do the will of the flesh, and of the devil:<sup class="proof-marker">e</sup> we pray, that God would by his Spirit take away from ourselves and others all blindness,<sup class="proof-marker">f</sup> weakness,<sup class="proof-marker">g</sup> indisposedness,<sup class="proof-marker">h</sup> and perverseness of heart;<sup class="proof-marker">i</sup> and by his grace make us able and willing to know, do, and submit to his will in all things, with the like humility,<sup class="proof-marker">l</sup> cheerfulness,<sup class="proof-marker">m</sup> faithfulness,<sup class="proof-marker">n</sup> diligence,<sup class="proof-marker">o</sup> zeal,<sup class="proof-marker">p</sup> sincerity,<sup class="proof-marker">q</sup> and constancy,<sup class="proof-marker">r</sup> as the angels do in heaven.<sup class="proof-marker">s</sup>

### Question 193: What do we pray for in the fourth petition?
Answer: In the fourth petition (which is, Give us this day our daily bread), acknowledging that in Adam, and by our own sin, we have forfeited our right to all the outward blessings of this life, and deserve to be wholly deprived of them by God, and to have them cursed to us in the use of them;<sup class="proof-marker">u</sup> and that neither they of themselves are able to sustain us,<sup class="proof-marker">w</sup> nor we to merit,<sup class="proof-marker">x</sup> or by our own industry to procure them;<sup class="proof-marker">y</sup> but prone to desire,<sup class="proof-marker">z</sup> get,<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup> and use them unlawfully:<sup class="proof-marker">b</sup> we pray for ourselves and others, that both they and we, waiting upon the providence of God from day to day in the use of lawful means, may, of his free gift, and as to his fatherly wisdom shall seem best, enjoy a competent portion of them;<sup class="proof-marker">c</sup> and have the same continued and blessed unto us in our holy and comfortable use of them,<sup class="proof-marker">d</sup> and contentment in them;<sup class="proof-marker">e</sup> and be kept from all things that are contrary to our temporal support and comfort.<sup class="proof-marker">f</sup>

### Question 194: What do we pray for in the fifth petition?
Answer: In the fifth petition (which is, Forgive us our debts, as we forgive our debtors), acknowledging that we and all others are guilty both of original and actual sin, and thereby become debtors to the justice of God; and that neither we, nor any other creature, can make the least satisfaction for that debt:<sup class="proof-marker">h</sup> we pray for ourselves and others, that God of his free grace would, through the obedience and satisfaction of Christ, apprehended and applied by faith, acquit us both from the guilt and punishment of sin,<sup class="proof-marker">i</sup> accept us in his Beloved; continue his favor and grace to us,<sup class="proof-marker">l</sup> pardon our daily failings,<sup class="proof-marker">m</sup> and fill us with peace and joy, in giving us daily more and more assurance of forgiveness;<sup class="proof-marker">n</sup> which we are the rather emboldened to ask, and encouraged to expect, when we have this testimony in ourselves, that we from the heart forgive others their offenses.<sup class="proof-marker">o</sup>

### Question 195: What do we pray for in the sixth petition?
Answer: In the sixth petition (which is, And lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil), acknowledging that the most wise, righteous, and gracious God, for divers holy and just ends, may so order things, that we may be assaulted, foiled, and for a time led captive by temptations;<sup class="proof-marker">q</sup> that Satan,<sup class="proof-marker">r</sup> the world,<sup class="proof-marker">s</sup> and the flesh, are ready powerfully to draw us aside, and ensnare us;<sup class="proof-marker">t</sup> and that we, even after the pardon of our sins, by reason of our corruption,<sup class="proof-marker">u</sup> weakness, and want of watchfulness,<sup class="proof-marker">w</sup> are not only subject to be tempted, and forward to expose ourselves unto temptations,<sup class="proof-marker">x</sup> but also of ourselves unable and unwilling to resist them, to recover out of them, and to improve them;<sup class="proof-marker">y</sup> and worthy to be left under the power of them; we pray, that God would so overrule the world and all in it,<sup class="proof-marker">a</sup> subdue the flesh,<sup class="proof-marker">b</sup> and restrain Satan,<sup class="proof-marker">c</sup> order all things,<sup class="proof-marker">d</sup> bestow and bless all means of grace,<sup class="proof-marker">e</sup> and quicken us to watchfulness in the use of them, that we and all his people may by his providence be kept from being tempted to sin;<sup class="proof-marker">f</sup> or, if tempted, that by his Spirit we may be powerfully supported and enabled to stand in the hour of temptation;<sup class="proof-marker">g</sup> or when fallen, raised again and recovered out of it,<sup class="proof-marker">h</sup> and have a sanctified use and improvement thereof:<sup class="proof-marker">i</sup> that our sanctification and salvation may be perfected, Satan trodden under our feet,<sup class="proof-marker">l</sup> and we fully freed from sin, temptation, and all evil, forever.<sup class="proof-marker">m</sup>

### Question 196: What doth the conclusion of the Lord's prayer teach us?
Answer: The conclusion of the Lord's prayer (which is, For thine is the kingdom, and the power, and the glory, forever. Amen.) teacheth us to enforce our petitions with arguments,<sup class="proof-marker">o</sup> which are to be taken, not from any worthiness in ourselves, or in any other creature, but from God;<sup class="proof-marker">p</sup> and with our prayers to join praises,<sup class="proof-marker">q</sup> ascribing to God alone eternal sovereignty, omnipotency, and glorious excellency;<sup class="proof-marker">r</sup> in regard whereof, as he is able and willing to help us,<sup class="proof-marker">s</sup> so we by faith are emboldened to plead with him that he would,<sup class="proof-marker">t</sup> and quietly to rely upon him, that he will fulfill our requests.<sup class="proof-marker">u</sup> And, to testify this our desire and assurance, we say, Amen
